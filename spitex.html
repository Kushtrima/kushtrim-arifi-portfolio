PROJECT

As always I began with user research to understand how patients and healthcare staff actually interact with Spitex services and where they face difficulties. With these insights, I designed the UX/UI in Figma, focusing on layouts, spacing, and typography that make navigation simple and reduce stress for users looking for support. A key part of the design was the teal color: in psychology it is seen as rare in everyday life, which makes it attention-catching, while in healthcare it is often used to communicate calmness, trust, and reliability. After the designs were approved, I built a lightweight, responsive website where services are easy to explore, patient information is accessible without confusion, and the contact form is straightforward—helping people get in touch quickly when they need it most.
